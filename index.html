HIT

This is a decision that a player makes to order for another card from the dealer
to make his hand a good hand by having a hand value of 17 or higher.
However if the dealer has a face-up card of 7 or higher the player should aim for a hand value
of 18 or higher

WHEN TO HIT

You should hit in black jack when your hand value is 11 or lower or when the dealers
face-up card is 7 or higher and your hand value is 12-16.(Hand signal- Tap the felt with your finger)

STAND

If your fisrt two cards are accepatable or with good hand value, you can stand
and the dealer will move to the next player.[Hand-signal: Wave your hand or simply put out
open palm over the felt.]

HOW TO PLAY

  1. Place your bet: Before the cards are dealt, players must place a bet. The minimum and maximum bets are usually
    posted on the table.
  2. Receive your cards: Once all bets have been placed, the dealer will deal two cards to each player, face up.
  3. Decide to hit or stand: After receiving your two cards, you can choose to “hit” and receive additional cards
    or “stand” and keep your current hand
  4. Dealer’s turn: After all players have had their turn, the dealer will reveal their face-down card and hit or
    stand according to predetermined rules.
  5. Determine the winner: If neither the player nor the dealer busts, the person with the highest hand value wins.

Game Play Techniques

Double Down

This is an option open to the player for doubling their bet when the original two cards dealt total 9, 10, or 11. When the player's turn comes, they place a bet equal to the original bet, and the dealer gives the player just one card, which is placed face down and is not turned up until the bets are settled at the end of the hand.

Surrendering

If you have an especially bad hand, you can "surrender" to the dealer instead of hitting or staying. Surrendering deducts 50% off your original bet, but it can save you from a total loss. To signal a surrender to the dealer, slide your pointer finger in a horizontal line on the game table. Note that not all casinos may allow surrendering.

Insurance

When the dealer's face-up card is an ace, any of the players may make a side bet of up to half the original bet that the dealer's face-down card is a ten-card, and thus a blackjack for the house. Once all such side bets are placed, the dealer looks at the hole card. If it is a ten-card, it is turned up, and those players who have made the insurance bet win and are paid double the amount of their half-bet – a 2 to 1 payoff. When a blackjack occurs for the dealer, the hand is over, and the players' main bets are collected – unless a player also has blackjack, in which case it is a stand-off. Insurance is generally not a good proposition for the player unless they are confident that there are an unusually high number of ten-cards still left undealt.
